## 🇬🇧 English

**Summary** — Many AI mistakes happen before the model writes an answer: the system has already selected the wrong evidence or action candidate. This idea improves reliability by making candidate selection more rule-aware, stable, and evidence-focused.

**What it does** — This feature changes reranking from simple similarity sorting into structured candidate comparison. Instead of asking which chunk looks closest, it checks whether each candidate directly answers the question, contains usable evidence, is up to date, and conflicts with other candidates. It also tests whether the same evidence is chosen when chunk text is partially hidden or candidate order is changed.

**How it helps AI capabilities** — This helps AI systems make fewer retrieval and grounding mistakes without requiring model retraining. It improves answer quality by reducing selection bias among long documents, similar chunks, and conflicting evidence. It also gives developers a measurable stability score for retrieval reliability.

**Suggested approach**
- Use comparison prompts that score candidates for direct answerability, evidence coverage, freshness, and contradiction risk.
- Test retrieval stability by masking parts of chunks, shuffling candidate order, and checking whether the same evidence is selected.
- Prefer candidates that remain stable across perturbations, not just candidates with high semantic similarity.
